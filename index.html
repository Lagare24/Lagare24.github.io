Bantayan Island

Bantayan Island is a small island off the coast of northern Cebu. Don`t be fooled by this sleepy fishing village, the beaches of this humble island exude the same charm and beauty as the famous Boracay Island. The best part is, Bantayan Island is not as commercialized and crowded as Boracay. If you want a relaxing vacation by the beach and witness one of the most beautiful sunsets, Bantayan Island won`t disappoint you. Bantayan Island is blessed with several sprawling white sand beaches and calm azure waters.

Most beaches on Bantayan Island are privately owned or privately owned by the resort, but are open to the public for a small fee. You do not need to check in at any resort to enjoy the beach. It is one of the best tourist attractions on Bantayan Island. Kota Beach on Bantayan Island is one of the best beaches in the Philippines. There are sandbars and lagoons that are perfect for playing in the water or sunbathing. Paradise Beach, on the other hand, is a serene stretch of white sand beach that features beautiful rock formations. Bantayan Island also boasts two natural cave pools, the Ogtong Cave and the Sto. Niño Cave. A natural cold spring feeds both caves, so you can expect chilly water that's great for cooling off after a morning of beachhopping.

Kawasan Falls

The most famous attraction in Badian is Kawasan Falls, a three-story waterfall with apparently turquoise water. There are two ways to explore the waterfall. One is to choose from a variety of canyoning escapes and it will take 3-6 hours by car from Alegria to Badian.

The other is a short hike to the waterfall. The final stage is the third and highest stage of Kawasan Falls or Kabukalan Spring, from which water flows. If you're not interested in canyoning, you can instead take part in a river mountain-only waterfall adventure.

Sirao Garden

Sirao Garden the picturesque flower garden, also known as Cebu's "Little Amsterdam," is located in the highlands of Busay. The colorful flowers of the Sirao Garden will surely make you want to take out your camera and take thousands of photos during your sightseeing tour of Cebu. The Sirao Garden is lined with sunflowers, hyacinths, chrysanthemums, daisies and asters. However, the main attraction is the bright and colorful cockscomb flowers. The Flower Farm first attracted the locals who wanted

to take pictures with beautiful flowers. But soon the news spread and many travelers began to take the Sirao Garden Tour. The Sirao Garden is lined with colorful vegetation, as well as replicas of Dutch windmills and observatories.

Temple of Leah

Celebrated as the Taj Mahal in Cebu, the Temple of Leah has become one of Cebu's most famous landmarks. This magnificent building is a spectacular sight and a popular Cebu activity with Instagram-worthy shots. If you like Cebu Hillside tours, the Temple of Leah is a great starting point. Located on the Busay Hills in Cebu City, the Temple of Leah is a towering seven-story building inspired by ancient Roman architecture. This magnificent building is said to cost more than 80 million pesos and has granite floors, Doric columns, marble fountains, and some Greek statues.

Osmeña Peak

The city of Dalaguete is known as the "Cebu Vegetable Basket" because it delivers crops throughout the state.
